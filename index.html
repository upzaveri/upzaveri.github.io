About

The person behind
the science.

I am currently a PhD candidate in theled by Prof. Paolo Sossi at ETH Zürich.

Originally from Mumbai, India, I completed my BSc in Geology at St. Xavier's College, then pursued an MSc in Earth Structure and Dynamics at Utrecht University. During my master's I helped establish the Utrecht Meteorite Lab, digitizing their meteorite collection using 3D photogrammetry.

This sparked my interest in meteorites and planet formation. Now my work focuses on rocky exoplanet interiors through thermodynamic modelling of protoplanetary disks, combined with high-pressure and high-temperature experiments.

My work lives at the intersection of geochemistry, high-pressure experiments, and planetary physics, asking what the inside of a rocky world really looks like when it forms somewhere very different from Earth.

When I'm not experimenting in the lab, I'm usually experimenting in my kitchen, spending time at the gym, dancing, or wandering through an art museum.

Research

How planets are
born and built.

My work spans multiple stages of planetary evolution, from the chemistry of protoplanetary disks and meteorites as records of early Solar System processes, to decoding the interior structure of rocky planets within and beyond the Solar System.

🔭 Ongoing Projects

Thermodynamic Modelling

Protoplanetary disk chemistry & planet accretion

Modelling disk chemistry with high C/O ratios using Gibbs free energy minimization, and simulating planet accretion to establish the star–planet connection for elements across chemically diverse planetary systems.

High-Pressure Experiments

High-pressure–high-temperature experiments

Piston-cylinder experiments to investigate phase stability in low oxygen fugacity systems, focusing on volatile element partitioning between metal and silicate liquids.

Geophysical Modelling

Interior structure of Mercury

Probing Mercury's interior by coupling experimentally constrained element partitioning laws with geophysical modelling to understand its internal structure and differentiation history.

🪨 Past Projects

Meteoritics

Ordinary chondrites and parent-body processes

Studied petrology and shock-related microtextures of the Utrecht L6 chondrite using RAMAN spectroscopy and Electron microprobe analysis.

Collaborators: Prof. Inge Loes ten Kate & Prof. Eric Hellebrand

Deep-Ocean Geochemistry

Iron nanoparticles in hydrothermal systems

Investigated iron nanoparticles from the Rainbow hydrothermal vent field, evaluating their stability and role in the deep-ocean iron cycle.

Collaborators: Prof. Oliver Plümper, Dr. Martina Preiner & Dr. Peter Kraal

Science Communication

Utrecht Meteorite Lab

As a Bright Minds Fellow, digitized Utrecht's meteorite collection via 3D photogrammetry. Models live on Sketchfab.

Collaborators: Prof. Inge Loes ten Kate & Dr. Sebastiaan de Vet

About Me

I am currently a PhD candidate in the Experimental Planetology Group led by Prof. Paolo Sossi.

I am originally from Mumbai, India, where I completed my BSc in Geology at St. Xavier’s College. I later pursued an MSc in Earth Structure and Dynamics at Utrecht University in the Netherlands. During my master’s studies, I was involved in establishing the Utrecht Meteorite Lab, where I worked on digitizing the meteorite collection of Utrecht University using 3D photogrammetry.

This experience sparked my interest in meteorites and planet formation more broadly. Currently, my work focuses on exploring rocky exoplanet interiors through thermodynamic modeling of protoplanetary disks, combined with high-pressure and high-temperature experimental techniques.

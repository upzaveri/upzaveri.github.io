I am currently a PhD candidate in the Experimental Planetology Group led by Prof. Paolo Sossi at ETH Zürich.

Originally from Mumbai, India, I completed my BSc in Geology at St. Xavier's College, then pursued an MSc in Earth Structure and Dynamics at Utrecht University. During my master's I helped establish the Utrecht Meteorite Lab, digitizing their meteorite collection using 3D photogrammetry.

This sparked my interest in meteorites and planet formation. Now my work focuses on rocky exoplanet interiors through thermodynamic modelling of protoplanetary disks, combined with high-pressure and high-temperature experiments.

My work lives at the intersection of geochemistry, high-pressure experiments, and planetary physics, asking what the inside of a rocky world really looks like when it forms somewhere very different from Earth.

When I'm not experimenting in the lab, I'm usually experimenting in my kitchen, spending time at the gym, dancing, or wandering through an art museum.

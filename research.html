Research

My research spans multiple stages of planetary evolution, from the formation of solids in protoplanetary disks and the study of meteorites as records of early Solar System processes, to decoding the interior structure of rocky planets within and beyond the Solar System.

A central theme of my work is understanding how elements partition between different planetary reservoirs under varying chemical and physical conditions.

I approach these questions by combining thermodynamic modeling, high-pressure–high-temperature experiments, and geophysical modeling.

Ongoing Projects

  • Protoplanetary disk chemistry and planet accretion — Modeling disk chemistry with high C/O ratios and simulating planet accretion.
  • High-pressure–high-temperature experiments — Piston-cylinder experiments in low oxygen fugacity systems.
  • Interior structure of Mercury — Coupling experimental partitioning laws with geophysical modeling.

Past Projects

  • Ordinary chondrites and parent-body processes
  • Iron nanoparticles in hydrothermal systems
